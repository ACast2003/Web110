Meta Threats

In the current meta theres a couple other powerful deck to watch out for

  1. Maliss
  2. Blue eyes White Dragon
  3. Fiendsmith Control

Knowing what they do and how to beat them can be the difference between winning and losing

Maliss

Malis is a flexible combo deck that utilizes cyberse link monsters and the banishment zone to create value out of link decoder and set up a powerful endboard with traps, recursion, and powerful link monsters. Maliss's playstyle of banishing their own cards for value makes it very hard to disrupt as there are not many ways to interact with banished cards. Maliss' primary weakness is to "lingering" hand traps or handtraps that apply an effect that lasts the whole turn. Artifact Lancea is a hand trap that disables the ability to banish cards for a turn. This can totally shut down Maliss's combo lines. Droll and Lock bird is another good lingering hand trap not quite as much of a hose against Maliss as they can play though it with a good enough opening hand it doubles as an anti Maxx "C" tech on your own turn as you are able to combo through it. Other than that there are few ways to beat maliss going second it is the undisputed best deck right now for a reason.

Blue Eye

Blue Eyes White Dragon is midrange combo deck capible of setting up powerful end boards thanks to their link one monster and sustaining a long game thanks to the primite engine. Blue eyes monsters are very hard to out if they stick. Blue eyes however has two key cards that are part of all of their combos that can be hit with common hand traps to keep them off getting two spirit dragons on the field. The first is the aformentioned link one "Spirit With Eyes of Blue", this monster is how they begin most combos and is vulnerable to "Infinite Impermanence" and effect veiler both common handtraps that negate a monsters effect. Blue eyes lacks a way to tag it out to dodge the removal as the tribute is not on a quick effect. The second big hit is to save ash blossom for "Wishes with Eyes of Blue" a powerful spell that adds both a spell and a light tuner to hand but costs a discard to activate, this means negating it is a two for one denying them lots of resources.

Fiendsmith Control

Fiendsmith control takes the already powerful recursive engine of fiendsmith and super charges it by playing it alongside a prismite package for dipity a, level 4 light fiend normal monster, and the bystials, a collection of maindeck handtrap dragons that stop graveyard decks that use light and dark monsters. This deck will never run out of resources so defeating it quickly is a must, Luckily mementotlan is good at one turn killing. The decks biggest weak points in their combo are standard fiendsmith targets, ash blossom on the link one and imperm on the link 2. This deck isnt as big of a threat to mementotlan however its prevalence means you should be prepared for it.
